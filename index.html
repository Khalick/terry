Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about nutrition, services, and consultations

What is the difference between a dietitian and a nutritionist? +
A Registered Dietitian Nutritionist (RDN) has completed accredited education, supervised practice, passed a national examination, and maintains continuing education. RDNs are qualified to provide medical nutrition therapy and work with complex health conditions. Nutritionists may have varying levels of training and credentials.
How long is a typical consultation? +
Initial consultations typically last 60-90 minutes to allow for a comprehensive nutritional assessment. Follow-up sessions are usually 30-45 minutes. We take time to understand your health history, lifestyle, and goals to create a personalized nutrition plan.
Do you offer virtual/online consultations? +
Yes! I offer both in-person consultations in Thika and Nairobi, as well as virtual consultations via WhatsApp video call or phone. Virtual sessions are just as effective and convenient for clients across Kenya and beyond.
What health conditions do you specialize in? +
I specialize in diabetes management, cardiovascular disease, weight management, gastrointestinal disorders, PCOS, metabolic syndrome, renal disorders, and sports nutrition. I also provide general wellness nutrition counseling and preventive health guidance.
Will I need to follow a strict diet? +
No! My approach focuses on sustainable, personalized strategies rather than restrictive diets. We work together to create a nutrition plan that fits your lifestyle, cultural preferences, and food choices while achieving your health goals. No extreme restrictions or one-size-fits-all approaches.
How soon will I see results? +
Results vary depending on your individual goals and health conditions. Many clients notice improvements in energy levels and digestion within 2-4 weeks. Significant changes in weight, blood sugar, or cholesterol typically occur within 2-3 months with consistent adherence to the nutrition plan.
Do you accept health insurance? +
I work with several health insurance providers in Kenya. Please contact me directly to discuss your specific insurance coverage. I can provide detailed receipts and documentation for insurance reimbursement purposes.
What should I bring to my first appointment? +
Please bring any recent lab results (blood tests, cholesterol panels, HbA1c), a list of current medications and supplements, your health history, and specific health goals. If you keep a food diary, that's also helpful! Don't worry if you don't have everything - we'll work with what you have.
